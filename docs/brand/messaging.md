## 1. Taglines

A tagline rides under the wordmark in a header or sits as a one-line answer to *"what is this?"* These are short, repeatable, and load-bearing.

### Lead (primary)

> **Audit-ready supervision. Every hour, every state, every signature.**

This is the line for the header, footer, and homepage hero subtitle. It names the category (supervision), the standard (audit-ready), and the scope (every hour / every state / every signature) without claiming anything we can't prove.

### Alternates (use where the lead is too long)

- **Supervision compliance, sealed.**
- **The calm command center for clinical supervision.**
- **Built for state-board audits, not against them.** *(already used in the marketing footer copyright line — keeps as a values line)*
- **Compliance is the halo that appears when supervision is geometrically correct.** *(the brand-book line; reserve for About / values pages, not marketing CTAs)*

### One-word / signet uses

- **Audit-ready.** *(used as a badge on roster rows, evidence packages, state cards)*
- **Sealed.** *(used as a one-word badge on completed evidence packages)*

## 2. Elevator pitches

### 10-second (one breath, networking, casual)

> AuditHalo turns a supervisor's roster into an audit-ready evidence package — one dashboard, every supervisee, every state rule, current.

### 30-second (warm intro, sales call open)

> Pre-licensed counselors — LCMHCAs, APCCs, LPC-Associates, RMHCIs — accrue thousands of supervised hours toward licensure, and the supervisor is personally liable to the state board for every one of them. Today most of that runs on spreadsheets. AuditHalo gives the supervisor one dashboard for their whole roster, a state-specific rules engine that flags gaps in real time, and a sealed, citation-grounded PDF evidence package per signed session. So when the audit shows up, the answer is already in a folder.

### 2-minute (investor / partner / supervisor demo intro)

> There are roughly 100,000 to 200,000 pre-licensed mental-health counselors in the United States, each one earning supervised hours toward an LPC, LCSW, LMFT, or LPCC license. The rules vary by state — North Carolina is 3,000 hours over two to five years, California is 3,000 hours over at least 104 weeks with 1,750 of direct contact, Florida's intern registration is 60 months non-renewable, Texas wants a monthly supervision cadence with at least one individual hour, New York runs on a Limited Permit with a hard expiration. None of this is in the EHRs. SimplePractice and TherapyNotes handle co-signatures on clinical notes; they do not produce a state-board-specific audit package keyed to the right admin-code citation.
>
> AuditHalo does. The supervisor is the buyer because the supervisor carries board liability and budget authority. The supervisee account is free — they're tomorrow's paying supervisor. Our moat is a curated, versioned, citation-grounded rules engine that we keep current; nothing commercial sells that today. Pricing is $89/month for a solo supervisor and $25/seat for a practice. We launched on Neon and Vercel, will move to BAA-eligible infrastructure when the first practice customer demands it, and have a 30-month roadmap from supervisor-led launch to enterprise.
>
> The wedge sentence we keep coming back to: every EHR helps you do the work. AuditHalo proves you did the work.
